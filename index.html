Tourism in Malaysia

An overview of domestic and inbound tourism statistics in Malaysia.

Visitor Arrivals

The line chart shows a dramatic decline in visitor arrivals in year 2020 & 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, where travel restrictions caused a drop of nearly 80%. Recovery began in year 2022 with border reopenings and increased international tourism. By year 2024, visitor arrivals nearly returned to pre-pandemic levels, signaling a strong rebound for Malaysia’s tourism sector.

Inbound Visitor Arrivals by Country

The data shows Malaysia’s inbound tourism trends from 2019 to 2023. Visitor arrivals peaked in 2019, led by Singapore, Indonesia, and China. Numbers dropped sharply in 2020–2021 due to COVID-19 travel restrictions, hitting their lowest in 2021. Tourism rebounded in 2022 and surged in 2023, with Singapore again leading by a large margin. Overall, the dataset highlights the pandemic’s severe impact and Malaysia’s strong post-pandemic recovery in regional tourism.

Transport Modes Used by Inbound Visitors

From 2019 to 2023, land transport was the main mode for visitors to Malaysia, making up over half of arrivals each year. Air travel ranked second, peaking in 2021 due to pandemic restrictions. Sea and rail transport contributed minimal shares, staying below 8% and 2% respectively. Overall, Malaysia’s inbound travel relied mostly on land and air connections, with temporary shifts during COVID-19.

Domestic Visitors by State

From 2019 to 2023, Selangor consistently recorded the highest number of domestic visitors, while Labuan and Putrajaya had the lowest. Visitor numbers dropped sharply in 2020–2021 due to the pandemic but rebounded strongly from 2022 onward.

Accommodation Types Used by Domestic Visitors

From 2019 to 2023, most domestic visitors in Malaysia stayed at relatives’ or friends’ houses, though this preference declined from 70.9% in 2019 to 61.3% in 2023. Hotels were the second most popular choice, increasing to 29.5% in 2021 before slightly dropping. Other options like rest houses, chalets, and homestays remained less common, with homestays showing gradual growth, peaking at 6.7% in 2023.

Tourism Expenditure Component
