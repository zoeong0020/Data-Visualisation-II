Tourism in Malaysia

An overview of domestic and inbound tourism statistics in Malaysia.

Visitor Arrivals

This line chart displays the overall visitor arrivals to Malaysia from 2019 to 2024. Hover over any point on the chart to view the exact number of visitor arrivals for each year.

Visitor arrivals dropped sharply in 2020 and 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, when travel restrictions caused an almost 80% decline. Recovery began in 2022 with border reopenings and growing international tourism.

Inbound Visitor Arrivals by Country

This horizontal bar chart shows the inbound visitors by country to Malaysia from 2019 to 2023. Select a year from the dropdown menu below the chart to visualize the data.

Visitor arrivals peaked in 2019, led by Singapore, Indonesia, and China. Numbers dropped sharply in 2020–2021 due to COVID-19 travel restrictions, hitting their lowest in 2021. Tourism rebounded in 2022 and surged in 2023, with Singapore again leading by a large margin.

Transport Modes Used by Inbound Visitors

This donut chart shows the modes of transport used by inbound visitors to Malaysia from 2019 to 2023. Select a year from the dropdown menu below the chart to visualize the data.

From 2019 to 2023, land transport remained the main mode for visitors to Malaysia, making up over half of arrivals each year. Air travel ranked second, peaking in 2021 due to pandemic restrictions. Sea and rail transport contributed minimal shares, staying below 8% and 2% respectively.

Domestic Visitors by State

This proportional symbol map shows the number of domestic visitors by state in 2019 to 2023. Scroll over the bar below the map to change the year and visualize the data.

From 2019 to 2023, Selangor consistently recorded the highest number of domestic visitors, while Labuan and Putrajaya had the lowest. Visitor numbers dropped sharply in 2020–2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic but rebounded strongly from 2022 onward.

Accommodation Types Used by Domestic Visitors

This stacked bar chart shows the accommodation preferences of domestic visitors in Malaysia from 2019 to 2023. Hover over any section of the stacked bars to view the exact percentage for each accommodation type in each year.

From 2019 to 2023, most domestic visitors stayed at relatives’ or friends’ houses, Hotels were the second most popular choice, increasing to 29.5% in 2021 before slightly dropping.

Tourism Expenditure Component

These two pie charts compare inbound and domestic visitors in terms of where they spent their money while visiting Malaysia from 2019 to 2023. You can change the year by selecting a different option from the drop-down menu below the charts.

Inbound expenditure dropped sharply in 2020 and 2021 due to pandemic restrictions, particularly in accommodation and transport services. However, a strong recovery began in 2022 and accelerated in 2023, with tourism characteristic goods and passenger transport leading the rebound.
